Abstract

L-systems, or Lindenmayer systems, are often used to create realistic looking artificial plant environments. While conceptually simple, L-systems have been extended with many features to create realistic plants models based on a description of the plant growth patterns. Significant progress has been made in providing robust frameworks for modeling artificial plants, but the process of creating specific plants species is still very much a manual process that often requires a deep understanding of the tools used.

In this project we will explore an alternate approach to plant species creation using genetic algorithms to automatically evolve plants suitable to particular environments.

By tuning environmental parameters for the availability of water, the amount of sunlight, and the ambient soil conditions, artificial plant species are produced which resembling physical plants that may grown in similar environments.

With this approach to plant species generation, the underlying L-system production rules are evolved based on random mutation combined with a fitness function to determine plant survival rates. In addition to the L-systems, several additional parameters are introduced to control plant growth and simulation at a more abstract level.

Normal environment parameters

Standard simulation using 50% values for all environmental parameters. A variety of plants evolve at random.

Barren environment

Simulation of a barren environment with low soil nutrient levels. The simulation starts with default parameters after which the nutrient level is decreased. Consequently the plant species become smaller and simpler, appearing grass like. Later the nutrient levels are increased slightly and the sunlight level is decreased as well resulting in the evolution of leafy brush like plants.

Dry environment

Simulation of a dry environment with low water levels. The initially plant species quickly loose their leaves and begin to grow thick stems which could be used for water storage.

Shady environment

Simulation of a shady environment with medium nutrient levels. Selectional pressure favors large leafy plants which can collect the most light resulting in a dense forested environment.
